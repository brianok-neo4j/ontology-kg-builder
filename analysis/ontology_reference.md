### `AdministrativePenalty`

The notice must afford the public at least 30 days to submit comments unless the Minister has lawfully shortened that period. The Minister may shorten the period if, in the Minister's opinion, the urgency of the situation requires it, the proposed regulation clarifies the intent or operation of the Act or regulations, or the proposed regulation is of a minor or technical nature. The Minister may also decide that the entire consultation process (subsections (1) to (5)) should not apply to a particular regulation-making power on the same three grounds — in which case those subsections do not apply and the Minister must give public notice of that decision as soon as reasonably possible. ### `Sanction`

A penalty, fine, remedy, or enforcement action for non-compliance — including dismissal, discipline, suspension, imposition of a penalty, intimidation, coercion, or harassment of any person as a form of retaliation for making a disclosure, and including penalties substituted by a regulatory tribunal where an original discharge or discipline is found to have been for cause but no specific contractual penalty applies. Also includes the quasi-criminal penalty regime for individual offenders under s.192 of the governing Act: (a) for a first offence, a fine of not more than $200,000 or imprisonment for not more than 12 months, or both; (b) for a subsequent offence, a fine of not more than $400,000 or imprisonment for not more than 12 months, or both. Special scales apply: (i) individuals convicted of an offence under s.28 (zero-tolerance of abuse) are liable to a fine of not more than $200,000, regardless of offence number; (ii) individuals convicted by virtue of s.75 who are members of a s.75(2) management body or directors/officers of a corporation that is the licensee of a non-profit long-term care home are liable to a fine of not more than $4,000; in every other case under s.75, the scale is $200,000 for a first offence and $400,000 for a second or subsequent offence; (iii) corporations convicted of an offence under the Act are liable to a fine of not more than $500,000 for a first offence and not more than $1,000,000 for a subsequent offence. In addition to any other penalty, a convicting court may order compensation or restitution to any person who suffered a loss as a result of the offence, and may prescribe probation-type conditions under s.72(3)(c) of the Provincial Offences Act even where the offence is not punishable by imprisonment.
