A board, committee, or governing body established by statute or agreement to manage or maintain a regulated facility on behalf of one or more municipalities or other public bodies. Examples: board of management maintaining a long-term care home, joint board established under inter-municipal agreement.

### `Court`
A judicial body or statutory administrative tribunal empowered to hear appeals on questions of law or fact, affirm or rescind decisions of administrative bodies, refer matters for rehearing, and exercise supervisory jurisdiction over regulated proceedings. Examples: Divisional Court, Superior Court of Justice, Court of Appeal, Ontario Land Tribunal.

### `InstitutionalActor`
An abstract intermediate category for Party subtypes that are formal institutions — bodies or organisations created or recognised by statute, agreement, or judicial authority to perform a defined public function. Subtypes include Court (judicial adjudication), RegulatoryBody (regulatory and quality-assurance oversight), and AdministrativeBody (management or governance of a regulated facility). Distinguished from Party in general, which also covers natural persons, residents, families, and municipalities acting as general stakeholders.

- **Subtype of:** `Party`
- **Subtypes:** `AdministrativeBody`, `Court`, `RegulatoryBody`

### `NormativeProvision`
An abstract category representing any provision of a legal instrument that prescribes conduct: a duty imposed on a party (Obligation), a restriction or ban on conduct (Prohibition), or an entitlement conferred on a party (Right). NormativeProvisions collectively form the behavioural layer of a regulatory framework — specifying what regulated parties must do, must refrain from doing, or are permitted to do.

- **Subtypes:** `Obligation`, `Prohibition`, `Right`
- **Outgoing:** `GOVERNED_BY` → `LegalInstrument`
